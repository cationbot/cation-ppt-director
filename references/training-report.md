# Anonymized Cation PPT Training Report

This report preserves reusable Cation-style PPT patterns while removing personal names, company names, local file paths, and raw business-sensitive page content.

## Summary

- Training source type: high-weight submitted decks and matching process drafts.
- Training size: 8 submitted decks, 144 total pages, 138 content pages.
- Purpose: learn how rough business materials are compressed into submit-ready, management-facing PPT pages.

- Result-number title: 57
- Theme-conclusion title: 50
- Judgment-conclusion title: 16
- Path-planning title: 12
- Stage-description title: 3

## Reusable Page Patterns

### Operating Review Pages

- Start with KPI completion or core metric movement.
- Put the business judgment in the title, not only in the body.
- Use the body to explain which segment, action, or project drove the movement.
- Keep one page focused on one core judgment.

### Planning Pages

- Start with target gap and current-state diagnosis.
- Use the title to state the path, not just the topic.
- Separate short-term actions, medium-term mechanism, and resource/risk asks.

### Project Retrospective Pages

- Explain background, problem, solution, launch timing, post-launch performance, remaining issue, and next action.
- Avoid turning retrospectives into long process records.
- Compress implementation details unless they prove the result.

### Data Pages

- Use `result + number` as the page logic.
- Show the main number clearly, then explain denominator, comparison baseline, and segment scope.
- Hide or reduce excessive chart labels when they do not support the conclusion.

## Draft-To-Submit Transformation Patterns

- Submitted decks often compress large process-draft pools into a smaller, more judgmental structure.
- Weak pages are deleted when they lack data support, repeat another page, or do not carry a clear conclusion.
- Similar pages are merged when they describe the same business movement or action logic.
- Overloaded pages are split when they carry too many claims or more than two core charts/tables.
- Titles are rewritten from neutral labels into conclusion-bearing statements.
- Explanation, metric scope, and implementation detail move into body text or notes after the title carries the point.
- Formatting is standardized across cards, tables, titles, and section headers.

## Cation-Style Defaults

- Restrained business tone over visual showcase.
- Conclusion-led titles.
- Light background and clear hierarchy.
- Meaningful density: full but not crowded.
- Strong data-story linkage.
- Explicit uncertainty when metrics do not support a broad claim.
- Approval pause before changing the user's point of view, deleting substantive content, or adding unsupported management judgments.

## Privacy Note

Do not restore personal paths, employee names, company names, or raw confidential page content into this skill. If concrete examples are needed, request task-local examples from the user and use them only for the current task unless the user explicitly asks to update the skill.
